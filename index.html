The Ultimate NFPA 96 Compliance Checklist for Restaurants

Oct 15, 2023 • Admin

Running a commercial kitchen comes with immense responsibility, and fire safety should always be at the top of the list. The National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) established the NFPA 96 standards to outline the minimum fire safety requirements related to the design, installation, operation, inspection, and maintenance of all public and private commercial cooking operations.

Why NFPA 96 Matters

Failure to comply with NFPA 96 isn't just a matter of failing a local health inspection. If a fire breaks out in your kitchen and investigators determine that your exhaust system was not maintained to NFPA 96 standards, your insurance company can deny your claim entirely. This leaves you personally liable for damages.

Your Quick Checklist

  • Schedule Regular Professional Cleanings: Based on your cooking volume (Monthly, Quarterly, Semi-Annually, or Annually). The cleaners must provide a certificate of performance.
  • Maintain Baffle Filters: Check filters daily. They should be cleaned regularly by staff to ensure proper airflow, but the deep ductwork must be left to pros. Never run the system with missing filters.
  • Ensure Access Panels are Present: NFPA 96 requires access panels on ductwork every 12 feet and at every change of direction to allow for thorough cleaning and inspection.
  • Hinge Your Roof Fan: The exhaust fan must be hinged so cleaners can tip it back to clean the fan blades and the vertical duct drop without damaging the electrical wiring or the roof.
  • Check Grease Catchers: The rooftop grease containment system should be emptied or have its pads replaced regularly to prevent grease from destroying the roof material (which is a massive fire hazard).
